## Design Principles

### 1. Mobile-First, Always
80%+ of restaurant guests search from phones. We design for a 375px screen first, then scale up.

### 2. Indian Context Built In
- Veg / non-veg badges (green/red squares — the universal standard)
- ₹ symbol and Indian price formatting
- Timings in IST
- WhatsApp as primary contact channel
- Regional language support ready

### 3. Speed = Revenue
A 1-second delay in load time kills 7% of conversions. We target:
- LCP (Largest Contentful Paint) < 2.5s on mobile 4G
- No layout shifts (CLS < 0.1)
- Images served in WebP/AVIF format

### 4. Beautiful Typography
We use **Cormorant Garamond** for headings — the same font used by Madras Square and Cafe Chai Coffee — because it makes food descriptions read like poetry.

### 5. Dark Luxury by Default
Deep charcoal backgrounds with warm cream text and gold accents. This palette:
- Photographs better in restaurant lighting
- Feels premium without feeling cold
- Reduces eye strain when browsing menus at night

## Questions to Ask Restaurant Clients

Use these in the first meeting:

1. "Who is your typical guest — age group, occasion, walk-in or delivery?"
2. "What do you want them to do when they land on your site — book a table, see the menu, order online?"
3. "Do you have events or a banquet space we should highlight?"
4. "What's your biggest frustration with your current website (or why don't you have one)?"
5. "Do you handle enquiries on WhatsApp or email?"
6. "Do you want to update the menu yourself, or are you okay calling us?"
7. "What restaurants do you admire the look of — in India or globally?"
